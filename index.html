The Problems with Traditional Monitoring

Current elderly monitoring solutions are intrusive, expensive, and full of limitations

Sensor Overload

Traditional systems require multiple sensors in every room - motion sensors, pressure mats, door sensors, and more. This creates a complex, expensive setup that's difficult to maintain.

  • High installation costs
  • Frequent battery replacements
  • Complex maintenance

Camera Blind Spots

Cameras can't see through walls, doors, or furniture. They miss critical areas like bathrooms, behind furniture, or during nighttime when lights are off.

  • Privacy concerns in bathrooms/bedrooms
  • Limited coverage areas
  • Poor performance in low light

Privacy Invasion

Cameras and audio recording devices invade personal privacy, making seniors feel constantly watched and uncomfortable in their own homes.

  • Loss of dignity and independence
  • Security risks from hacked cameras
  • Psychological discomfort

False Alarms

Traditional systems generate frequent false alarms from pets, visitors, or normal movement patterns, leading to alert fatigue and missed real emergencies.

  • Caregiver alert fatigue
  • Missed real emergencies
  • Unnecessary emergency calls

Limited Mobility Tracking

Wearable devices can be forgotten, removed, or lost. They don't provide comprehensive mobility analysis or early warning signs of health decline.

  • Devices forgotten or removed
  • No comprehensive mobility analysis
  • Limited battery life

Complex Installation

Most systems require professional installation, drilling holes, running wires, and complex configuration that disrupts the home environment.

  • Professional installation required
  • Home disruption during setup
  • Difficult to relocate or expand
